Performance Analysis of Moving Average Crossover Strategies in High-Frequency Trading Environments

This research publication, "Discrete Signal Processing in Financial Time-Series," analyses the efficacy of the Dual-Moving Average Crossover (DMAC) algorithm through a computer science lens. By treating market prices as a high-dimensional data stream, the paper details the construction of a Python-based backtesting engine optimised via vectorised operations. The study evaluates strategy performance across multiple asset classes using risk-adjusted metrics like the Sharpe Ratio, ultimately demonstrating how algorithmic low-pass filters can mitigate emotional bias and isolate tradable trends in volatile markets.
